Germany • Details

A quick little Frankfurt day trip full of fresh air, river views, beer, and one very on-brand train mishap.

We only had one day in Germany, so we took the subway into Frankfurt’s city center and started walking. It was one of those simple travel days that ends up being more charming because of how unplanned it felt — sausages, beer, river views, architecture, and one very funny moment where we accidentally rode the train the wrong direction and just had to laugh about it.

Travel cost: $124.00

What we did, day by day

Day 1

Date: July 9, 2024

Main plan: Fly in from Paris, take the subway into Frankfurt, walk around the city center, eat a frankfurter sausage, drink some beer, and explore the river and architecture.

Best part: The whole day had such an easy, airy feel to it — lots of walking, beautiful weather, and a city that felt nice to simply move through.

Worth repeating? Yes

Little moment I do not want to forget

Memory: Aiden getting yelled at by a German man for walking along the river felt hilariously on-brand, and the train accidentally reversing directions on us is still one of the funniest moments from our travels.

Why it mattered: Those tiny weird moments always end up making a place feel more alive in memory.

Worth keeping? Absolutely

Favorites

Best part: Walking through the city center with no real rush

Best food memory: Eating a real frankfurter in Frankfurt

Best little moment: The accidental reverse train ride that somehow became one of our funniest travel memories

Best overall feeling: A beautiful day with fresh air, river views, and a city that was easy to explore on foot.

What I’d recommend

If you only have a day, head straight into the city center and walk as much as you can. Grab something local, sit for a beer, follow the river a little, and let the day stay simple. Frankfurt worked well for that kind of easy exploring.

What I’d skip

The only thing I would skip is assuming the train situation is obvious. We figured it out eventually, but not before accidentally giving ourselves a very funny detour.
